# IELTS Speaking Test – Perfect Band 9

## Summary
A mock IELTS speaking test where an Indian candidate is examined across the three standard parts and ultimately scored Band 9 (the top score, achieved by under 1% of test-takers). The video doubles as a teaching example showing what high-level English speaking sounds like.

## Topics Covered
- **Part 1**: Music habits (listens while driving/working out, prefers Afro/hip-hop/R&B), music in Indian schools, live concerts.
- **Magazines vs. newspapers**: Prefers fashion and interior-design magazines for inspiration; still values physical books over digital.
- **Outdoor activities**: Loves football, swimming, beach; cricket and badminton are most popular in India.
- **Part 2 (cue card)**: Favorite actor and actress — Benedict Cumberbatch (loves him in *Sherlock* and *Doctor Strange*) and Angelina Jolie (admires her humanitarian work and "divine feminine" presence).
- **Part 3**: Discussion of popular movie genres in India, the future of cinemas, and theater as a public service vs. business.

## Examiner's Feedback (the teaching core)
- **Pronunciation**: Strong point. Clear, with strong intonation, connected speech, and natural sentence stress — features that take years to learn.
- **Fluency**: Good. Occasional pauses, but for idea-formation, not language struggle.
- **Coherence**: The one weak area. She sometimes answered questions implicitly rather than explicitly (e.g., when asked "how often" she described "when"). She also discussed both an actor AND actress when the cue card asked for one.
- **Grammar**: Excellent — only minor preposition/article slips comparable to a native speaker.
- **Vocabulary**: Excellent, with topic-specific and idiomatic language.

## Final Score
**Band 9** — with the caveat that under real exam pressure she should answer questions more explicitly to avoid being marked down.
