# Embarrassing Things That Shouldn't Be Embarrassing!! | ShxtsnGigs Podcast

## Summary
The ShxtsnGigs hosts (James and his co-host) riff comedically through a list of mundane situations that somehow trigger deep embarrassment. The episode is a stream of personal anecdotes built around the prompt: "What shouldn't be embarrassing but is?"

## Topics & Anecdotes
- **Getting caught talking to yourself**: One host nearly let out a verbal thought in a quiet car ride from LA, panicked when the other heard him.
- **Talking to the screen during football** — a confessional default.
- **Waving back at someone who isn't waving at you**: A "double-down" handshake fail at an event where he reached out to a stranger who was actually greeting the person behind him.
- **Card declined for security/fraud-protection reasons**: One host got declined three times at a Shoreditch hotel and had to use his personal card after the receptionist asked the dreaded "are you sure you don't want to use a different card?"
- **Flushing twice**: Long, scatological story about needing the toilet at David Lloyd gym after pre-workout + Red Bull, dealing with a wave-sensor flush that wouldn't trigger for a "doo-doo" — only "trickled" for a urine flush.
- **Realizing you're walking the wrong way in public** and having to turn around. Host got lost twice trying to find his car the same day in London.
- **Paying in coins** instead of notes.
- **Pressing "no tip" on a pickup order**: Sparks an extended rant about American tipping culture — Coachella food stands, an LA bartender at "Scopa" who tried to add his own tip, an airport bar tipping prompt despite the bartender doing nothing — they argue tax/tip should be baked into displayed prices.
- **Trying a new gym exercise** when you don't know the layout.
- **Being the new person at a job**: First-day migraines, the anxiety of repeating your name, induction lunches with "Social Butterflies" — one host walked out after two minutes calling it "fake."

## Tone
Crude, conversational British humor; lots of self-deprecating storytelling.
