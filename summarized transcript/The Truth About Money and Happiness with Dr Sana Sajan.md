# The Truth About Money and Happiness with Dr Sana Sajan

## Summary
A Dubai-based interview with Dr. Sana Sajan — doctor, philanthropist, entrepreneur, artist, and director at the Danube Group (the Dubai conglomerate run by her father-in-law Rizwan Sajan and husband Adel Sajan). She discusses her journey from medicine into business, womanhood, leadership, motherhood, and her relationship with money.

## Background
- Comes from a family of 36 doctors; chose medicine inspired by her father.
- In college, used scholarship money to pay for poor patients' treatments.
- Returned to Dubai, worked with thalassemia patients and the Genetic Disease Association.
- Joined Danube Welfare Centre after marriage to support blue-collar workers' education.
- Now runs American Aesthetics ("the happiness center") under Danube's healthcare arm.

## Core Philosophy
- **Women as "Devi"**: Compares women to a 10-handed goddess holding many tools — meaning women are inherently capable of multiple roles. Encourages keeping a bucket list with no time limits.
- **"Bigger than life" mindset**: Limitations are mental cages.
- **Two lives quote**: "We all live two lives — the one we're given, and the one we live when we realize that's the only life we have."

## Daily Routine
- Reviews tomorrow's calendar before bed.
- Wakes children, breakfasts together with the joint family, gym, work.
- Hard stop at 5:30 PM for the kids.
- Post-8 PM is reserved for friend visits and parties — frames every party as "a blessing."

## Leadership Style
- Empathetic over authoritarian — gives staff "three chances" before letting them go (acknowledges current best practice favors faster firing).
- Believes in clear targets, regular team meetings, troubleshooting challenges as a group.
- Customer service obsessed — one happy customer brings two more.
- Motherhood made her more empathetic and better at understanding individual team members.

## Money & Happiness
- Self-describes as "Sufi" — almost shaved her head and became a monk before marriage.
- Tells the Persian-saint parable: a saint dressed in rags was turned away from the king's feast; came back in golden robes and was welcomed, then fed the food to his clothes — "It wasn't me who was invited, it was my robes." Lesson: people judge books by their covers, but money is a tool, not a goal.
- Once you're past survival, more zeros stop changing your life — so direct money toward family, mental health, and community.
- Riches = sleep, recovery, friendships, family, team. Even sleeping is "productive" because you're recovering for tomorrow.

## On Challenges & Self-Care
- "A disease diagnosed well is half-solved" — define the problem before reacting.
- Models calm in emergencies: when her father was rushed to hospital at 3 AM, she calmly delegated to staff while driving without speeding.
- Story of the bison: bisons charge into the snowstorm because facing it is the fastest way out. Same for life challenges.
- Self-care priorities: food, water, sleep (consistent timing matters more than 8 hours), exercise (even 30 min).

## On Charity Being "Selfish"
From her TED Talk: giving money/love makes the giver feel good — so be selfish in that beautiful way and multiply happiness.

## Bucket List Now
A school for children who can't afford education — her current dream.

## Tone
Bubbly, inspirational, lifestyle-coach in register; Dubai-glamour aesthetic blended with spiritual undertones.
