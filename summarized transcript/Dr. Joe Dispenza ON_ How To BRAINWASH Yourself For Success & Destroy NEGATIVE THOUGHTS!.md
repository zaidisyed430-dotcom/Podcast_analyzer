# Dr. Joe Dispenza on How to "Brainwash" Yourself for Success & Destroy Negative Thoughts | On Purpose with Jay Shetty

## Summary
Jay Shetty interviews neuroscientist/author Dr. Joe Dispenza on how repetitive thoughts hardwire the brain, how meditation can rewrite the operating system, and the data Dispenza's team has gathered from week-long retreats showing biological and even disease-state changes.

## The Core Mechanism
- **90% of our daily thoughts are repeats of yesterday's.** They become beliefs because "neurons that fire together wire together."
- The same thoughts → same choices → same behaviors → same experiences → same emotions → which in turn reinforce the same thoughts. The body becomes "conditioned to be the mind."
- Negative loops (e.g., "I'm unworthy") create a **biological craving** for the familiar emotion. Breaking the loop feels physically uncomfortable because the body's receptor sites have been modified to expect that emotional chemistry.

## The Path of Change
1. **Make unconscious thoughts conscious** — refuse to default into them.
2. **Decide who you want to be** — install new circuits via repeated, intentional thought (prefrontal cortex assigns meaning).
3. **Mental rehearsal** — the brain doesn't distinguish between vividly imagined and real experiences. Rehearse how you want to act in your Zoom calls, traffic, with co-workers.
4. **Teach the body emotionally** — feel gratitude, abundance, or worthiness *before* the manifestation. The body, being objective, starts believing it lives in that new environment, signaling genes ahead of the actual environment.

## The Brain-Wave Model of Meditation
- **Beta**: thinking/analytical (most stressed-out adults live here, like driving in first gear).
- **Alpha**: door between conscious and subconscious mind opens — creative, image-based.
- **Theta**: hypnotic, suggestible — most change happens here.
- **Gamma**: super-conscious, super-aware — what happens when you "relax into the heart and awaken the brain."

Dispenza's retreats have measured "supernatural" amounts of coherent gamma — 200–400 standard deviations beyond normal.

## Heart Coherence
- In stress, the heart beats incoherently against constricted arteries. Brain and heart waves cancel each other out → less energy.
- Resting attention in the heart, slowing breath, feeling elevated emotions creates a measurable external magnetic field.
- Coherent brain (intention) + coherent heart (emotion) = signal in the field that "draws things to us."

## Data From the Retreats
- Studies on 1,000+ participants per event measure brainwaves, gene expression, 2,882 metabolites, DNA, urine, saliva, room energy.
- Drug studies: 18–25% causality. Dispenza's data: **75–85% causality**.
- Reported transformations: stage-4 cancers in remission, blind people seeing, deaf hearing, ALS reversals, spinal-cord-injured patients walking — sustained when participants maintain the new emotional state. Conditions return when people revert to old personality.
- Lab finding: blood from advanced meditators reduced mitochondrial function in cancer cells by ~70%.

## On Stress, Emergency, and Habit
- Stress hormones are useful in real emergencies but addictive. 70% of people live in chronic stress mode.
- "Sometimes you need emergency — don't make emergency a habit." (Jay's phrasing.)
- A single mismanaged hour of meditation cannot outweigh 15–16 hours of reactive emotional living. The work has to extend into eyes-open daily life.

## The "Random Event Generator" Research
At Dispenza's group healings, RNGs (sophisticated coin-flippers producing 1000 tosses/second) consistently break their statistical baseline 100% of the time — suggesting collective coherent observers shift "random" toward intentional.

## Practical Takeaways
- Forgiveness is a side effect of changing emotional state, not a forced act.
- Mode-of-goodness thoughts (Jay): "I can take steps daily to create love" beats both "I'm unworthy" (ignorance) and "I can have anyone I want" (passion).
- Habits crisis, not values crisis: people know what to change but lack the rewiring tools.
- Practice in many forms: sitting, walking, standing, lying down, eyes-open meditations so you don't default back when conditions change.
