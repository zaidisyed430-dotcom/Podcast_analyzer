# How Michael Phelps Built a Champion Mindset: Obsession & Discipline | FO458 Raj Shamani

## Summary
The most-decorated Olympian of all time sits with Raj Shamani for what is essentially a masterclass in elite-athlete mindset. Phelps walks through his early life, training under Bob Bowman, peak years (2008 Beijing — 8 golds), the 2016 comeback, and his ongoing post-retirement work on mental health.

## The Numbers
- 5 Olympic Games, 28 medals (23 gold), 39 world records.
- "Only 23 of those 28 count." Silver = lost. Bronze = lost.
- If Phelps was a country, he'd rank 3rd in all-time swimming medals.

## Childhood & ADHD
- Was constantly "bouncing off the wall," restless, multi-sport. Played baseball, lacrosse, soccer, plus swimming to burn energy.
- A teacher told him he'd "never amount to anything" — Phelps still remembers her name and where he sat. Used it as fuel.
- Water became his outlet — even now, when depressed, his wife "kicks him out of the house" to swim. The pool is the reset switch.

## Hating to Lose
"I hate to lose more than I enjoy winning." From age 9–10, second place was never an option. He believes this came partly from his athletic father (cut from the Washington Redskins). Preparation became the lever — he was simply more prepared than anyone else for 20 years.

## Coach Bob Bowman
- They were together for 25+ years; Bowman became a father figure.
- At age 11, Bowman told Phelps and his parents: "If he wants to make the Olympic team in four years, he can." Demanded he drop baseball, lacrosse, and soccer.
- **Six-beat kick discipline**: Bowman told 11-year-old Phelps that any time he dropped his legs into a non-six-beat kick during practice, he'd be kicked out of the pool. First day he made 500 yards. By end of week, a full 6,000-yard workout. Never dropped his feet again.
- Bowman would intentionally **break his goggles** before practice to teach him to carry backups — directly paid off in 2008 Beijing when goggles filled with water and Phelps had to count strokes blind to win and break a world record.
- Routine warmup: identical 8642 or 6442 pattern from age 13 until retirement.

## The 6-Year Streak
After realizing one day off equals two days lost in swimming, Phelps trained **every single day for six straight years** — no Christmas, no sick days. Daily routine: morning workout → eat plates → nap → eat → afternoon workout → eat → sleep → repeat.

## The Block Mindset
- "When I'm on the block, **nothing**. Zero. I don't think about my start, my breakout, my kicks." Just stretch, arm slap, go.
- Pre-race headphones: Lil Wayne, Eminem (*Till I Collapse*), Young Jeezy, Biggie. He'd start the song again every time he restarted any task and try to hit a specific spot before taking it off.
- "I'm a dog trying to get out of the cage. I'm the shark in the water — if I smell the smallest ounce of blood, I'm going to destroy you."

## The Famous 0.01-Second Win (2008, 100m fly vs. Cavic)
He didn't time the wall correctly — finished with a half-stroke that barely won by 1/100th. "If it had been 201m I would have lost." Cavic glided into the finish; Phelps hit it with force. The half-stroke was practiced 10,000+ times — when in the race, "you don't think, you just be."

## The Chad le Clos Stare-Down (Rio 2016)
The famous "Phelps Face" meme. Le Clos was shadow-boxing in the ready room to intimidate. Phelps spotted the camera red lights, locked in. "Focus on you, not on what others are doing. Don't try to intimidate other people — it only wastes your energy."

## The "WR in 6 Months" Note
After getting 5th in Sydney 2000 (youngest male US swimmer since 1932), Bowman wrote *WR* on the next workout sheet — "you're going to break a world record in six months." Six months later, Phelps did. That's when full trust between the two locked in.

## Suicidal Thoughts & Mental Health
- Has had multiple suicidal thoughts. Therapy saved his life.
- Suicide is the second leading cause of death for ages 10–34 — "saving a life is a thousand times bigger than winning an Olympic gold."
- His self-care checklist includes: cold plunge, infrared sauna, journaling, men's group, therapist call, lion's breath (loud roaring exhale), workout.
- Disagrees with the political claim that depression is solved by "working harder" — for him, hard work was the *cause* of compartmentalization that erupted later.

## "Phelps Protocol" — Three Principles
1. **Dream → Plan → Reach**. The dream alone is worthless without the plan.
2. **Action speaks louder than words.** His all-time favorite quote.
3. **Simplify and stack.** "It wasn't rocket science — just a lot of small things done well over time." 30 days creates a habit. Don't try to fix everything at once.

## Closing Theme
"What is the purpose of your job?" Phelps insists most challenges in life — fitness, parenting, depression — are solved by daily routines and presence, not motivation. He works out every day even when he doesn't want to, because 20 minutes in he's always glad he did.

## Tone
Direct, intense, surprisingly vulnerable when discussing mental health. Phelps repeatedly emphasizes that there was no secret — just exhaustive preparation no one else was willing to match.
