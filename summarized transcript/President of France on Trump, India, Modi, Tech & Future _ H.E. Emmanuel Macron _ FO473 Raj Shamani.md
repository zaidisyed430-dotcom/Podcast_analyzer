# Emmanuel Macron on Trump, India, Modi, Tech & Future | Raj Shamani – Figuring Out FO473

## Summary
French President Emmanuel Macron's first-ever podcast appearance, recorded in Mumbai while he is the chief guest at the Republic Day. Host Raj Shamani interviews him on France's tech ambitions, the India-France strategic partnership, multipolarity, and his views on the Trump administration.

## Why France Lags in Big Tech
Macron identifies three missing ingredients for Europe:
1. **Scale** — fragmented single market needs to fully unify (a 450M-person domestic market).
2. **Capital** — Europeans save more than Americans, but regulation channels savings into bonds rather than venture/tech.
3. **Risk appetite** — European founders need to be bolder.

## €109B AI Investment & Sovereignty
Of the €109B announced for AI infrastructure, ~€50B comes from UAE and the technology is largely American/Chinese. Macron defends this as pragmatic: data centers in France enable French players, force tech transfer, and ensure compliance with European regulations. France's Mistral AI is positioned as the European LLM alternative.

## France's Moonshot for 2035
**Quantum computing** — France has world-class mathematicians and high-performing labs/startups; Macron sees this as the field where France can take leadership.

## Pitch to Indian Founders & Students
- Best universities (Polytechnique, Paris-Saclay, ENS, top engineering schools) — taught in English.
- Mature large-company economy + Europe's most vibrant startup ecosystem.
- "French taste" — unique nexus of fashion, gastronomy, cinema, art and tech.
- Goal: raise Indian student count from 9,300 to 30,000.

## India-France Strategic Partnership
Macron praises India's DPI leapfrog (Aadhaar, UPI, DigiLocker), notes India trains more engineers per year than US and Europe combined. Cites concrete deliverables: Solar Alliance, IMEC corridor, AI Action Summit. Both countries share a "third way" approach — strong relations with US and China without dependence on either.

## On Trump & American Leadership
Without naming directly, Macron pushes back on Trump's public sharing of private messages and 200% tariff threats: **"Respect is part of leadership."** Says democracies should disagree without violence or disrespect. Criticizes leaders "going backwards" on civilizational norms. Calls the Trump administration anti-Europe.

## On the Dollar & Multipolarity
Dollar still dominates today — Macron is "super careful" about claiming otherwise. But he wants to strengthen the euro's international role through swap lines, more euro-denominated trade, and making it a safe/liquid alternative for central banks.

## What Scares Him / Gives Him Hope
- **Optimistic about**: more well-educated young people globally than ever before.
- **Worried about**: collapse of predictable defense/security framework after Russia's war on Ukraine; arms-race escalation; UN charter no longer treated as common code.
- He has 15 months left in his term and treats it as motivation, not fear.

## Closing
Macron praises India's trajectory and the podcast hosts; off-camera small talk reveals warmth and breaking-protocol habits (he jogged through Mumbai traffic that morning).

## Tone
Diplomatic but candid. Macron is direct on Trump and on Europe's failures while remaining optimistic.
