# Theo Von and Donald Trump Talk About Addiction and Sobriety

## Summary
Comedian Theo Von speaks with Donald Trump about why Trump has never had a drink, smoked, or used drugs. Trump explains the pivotal influence of his older brother Fred Trump Jr., who struggled with alcoholism and repeatedly warned Donald: "Don't ever drink, don't ever smoke." Theo, who is in recovery from drugs and alcohol himself (sober for over two years most recently), shares his own experience and asks Trump personal questions about Fred.

## Key Themes
- **The protective older brother**: Fred Trump Jr. was handsome, charismatic, and a talented pilot, but alcohol destroyed his life. Donald says he admired him deeply and credits Fred's repeated warnings for keeping him sober.
- **Alcohol vs. drugs**: Theo notes alcohol can be harder to quit than drugs because it's social and everywhere; Trump agrees it's pervasive at dinners and events.
- **"If you don't drink, you don't miss it"**: Trump's recurring message — abstaining from the start is far easier than quitting later.
- **Cautionary anecdote**: A Wharton classmate hated the taste of scotch but forced himself to drink it for business networking; he became an uncontrollable alcoholic and died.
- **Recovery as conversation**: Theo says discussing this on his podcast normalizes addiction talk for listeners struggling with alcoholism, drugs, and intimacy disorders.
- **Fred's resilience**: Donald recalls Fred almost dying five or six times before finally passing; he had to give up flying — which he loved — because of his drinking.
- **Brothers as "speed bumps"**: Theo reflects on how older brothers absorb hard lessons so younger ones don't have to.

## Tone
Reflective, personal, surprisingly candid for a political figure.
