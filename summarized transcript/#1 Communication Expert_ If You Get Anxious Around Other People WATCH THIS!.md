# #1 Communication Expert: If You Get Anxious Around Other People WATCH THIS! | Vinh Giang on On Purpose with Jay Shetty

## Summary
Jay Shetty interviews communication coach Vinh Giang — a former magician turned global speaking coach — on how to upgrade voice, body language, and presence. Vinh's central insight: communication is just a stack of *practiced behaviors*, not innate personality.

## Origin Story
Vinh grew up speaking Teochew (Chinese dialect) → Vietnamese → English, struggling to communicate. He pivoted from magic to teaching public speaking when applause started feeling empty and teaching felt fulfilling.

## Core Thesis: Voice as Habits
- "Your current voice isn't your natural voice — it's your *habitual* voice." You lost your natural voice by age 2–3 and inherited mannerisms from caretakers.
- Communication is a series of behaviors you've practiced for 15–40 years. New behaviors feel *unfamiliar*, not *fake*.

## The Four Stages of Learning (applied to speech)
1. Unconscious incompetence — you don't know what you're doing wrong.
2. Conscious incompetence — Vinh helps audiences reach this.
3. Conscious competence — knowing it but having to think; feels phony.
4. Unconscious competence — mastery, total freedom.

## Practical Tools

### Self-awareness ("Record and Review")
1. Record 5 minutes of yourself speaking.
2. Wait a day.
3. Watch on **mute** — note hands, face, legs, ticks.
4. Watch with audio off-screen — note rate, volume, melody, filler words.
5. Get the speech transcribed — notice circular speech, repetition.

### Why People Interrupt You
Low vocal presence + low physical presence make you "easy to interrupt." Stand up if possible, raise volume, use larger body language. People stop interrupting because friction increases.

### The Piano / Home Analogy
You currently play 5 keys of an 88-key instrument because those keys are "home." Don't be so attached to who you are that you don't give the future you a chance.

### Why You Hate Your Recorded Voice
Voice in your head travels through bone & muscle (denser medium → lower pitch). Recordings travel through air → thinner, higher. The fix: desensitize by listening repeatedly until calibration corrects.

### Introvert vs Extrovert
A pianist's playing is the same regardless. Introverts simply have to be **strategic** about when they "play their instrument" and need recovery rituals.

### Energy Management Menu
- Conserve energy before high-stakes events (Vinh stayed in his hotel room all morning, didn't speak to videographer).
- Wim Hof breath: 30 deep breaths, exhale and hold, inhale-hold 15s, repeat.
- Light snack (blueberries + cream).
- Favorite beverage (coffee).
- Funny videos saved for mood priming.
- Orchestral war music (Hans Zimmer-style).

### The Power of the Pause
"The reason people aren't comfortable with the pause is they don't know what it's for. The pause allows people to process what you're saying."

## Failure as Teacher
"You learn far more from your failures than your successes." Older students take longer to change because they have 30–40 years of behavior to unlearn — Vinh works on **one thing at a time** for 4 weeks (one CEO had to focus on volume alone for a month).

## Jay's Story
At 11, Jay's parents put him in 7 years of public-speaking classes (London Academy of Music, Drama and Arts) — exam format included unseen book reading, 15-minute prep talks, and prepared presentations with visuals. Those years of discomfort built his current confidence.

## Closing Mastery Idea
"When you feel something, think something, and you're immediately able to articulate it with clarity and make people laugh — that is freedom." Achievable for anyone willing to push through the discomfort of stage three.

## Tone
Practical, exercise-driven coaching session — clear playbook for anyone wanting to improve their voice, presence, and presentation.
