# The Future Mark Zuckerberg Is Trying To Build

## Summary
Episode 1 of "Huge Conversations" by Cleo Abram (creator of "Huge If True"). The host interviews Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg about Meta's hardware roadmap, AI strategy, and the future of human connection — built around the prototype "Orion" holographic AR glasses he hands her at the start.

## Orion: The Holographic AR Glasses
- 10 years of R&D; only a few thousand exist; called "real-life Tony Stark glasses."
- First full holographic AR in normal-glasses form factor with wide field of view.
- Architecture: micro-projectors fire light into wave-guide nano-etchings to create holograms; eye-tracking, cameras, microphones, speakers, and a wrist-based neural interface.
- Future use cases: full-body holographic calls, holographic chess/board games, education, productivity.

## The Four-Tier Hardware Strategy
Meta is building permanent product lines, not a single ladder:
1. **Display-less smart glasses** (Ray-Ban Meta) — affordable, voice + AI.
2. **Heads-up display glasses** — narrower 20–30° field of view; for AI text, navigation, messaging.
3. **Full holographic AR (Orion)** — premium tier.
4. **Mixed reality headsets** (Quest 3 / Quest 3S at $299) — most compute, full immersion.

## Two Main Value Propositions
1. **Presence** (AR/MR side) — making people feel physically present with each other across distance.
2. **Personalized AI** — context-aware AI that "sees what you see and hears what you hear." Glasses are the ideal form factor for this.

## On Haptics & Physical Connection
Hands-first haptics will progress (controllers already give pingpong-paddle feedback); full force-feedback (e.g., for jiu-jitsu) is hardest. Smell — important for memory — won't arrive in devices anytime soon. The illusion of presence breaks easily: any single thing done wrong (low FOV, bad latency, unrealistic physics) ruins it.

## Loneliness & Social Media
Cleo cites US data: time socializing in person down ~30% in 20 years; 70% for ages 15–24; share of Americans with no close friend went from 3% to 12%. Zuckerberg's response: digital connection isn't replacing physical connection — most people already want more friends than they have. Glasses won't reduce time with his wife; they'll increase time with his sister across the country.

## On Open Source AI
Meta's bet (Llama) is the opposite of OpenAI/Google's "one AI for everything." Zuckerberg expects every business and creator to have their own AI. Open source historically makes software safer because more people can scrutinize it — same will apply to AI models.

## On AI & Social Media Future
- Friends will create funnier content with AI-generated tools.
- AI may auto-edit captured glasses footage.
- Creators will deploy AI versions of themselves to interact with fans at scale.
- Pure AI-generated content (summaries, personalized humor) will emerge.

## On AI & Skill Atrophy
Coding and language are like calculators-for-this-generation: kids should still learn them because they teach rigorous thinking and cultural understanding, even when AI can do the production. With "perfect translation" (just announced for Ray-Ban Meta in select languages), priorities about what to learn will shift.

## The Open Research Question on His Mind
Will the current transformer-based scaling laws keep paying off? Meta is betting hundreds of billions on the assumption they will (Llama 3 → Llama 4 → Llama 5 with 100K+ GPU training). If they hit a plateau, the world looks very different.

## Tone
Optimistic founder talking shop; Cleo pushes back on loneliness data and on whether human "struggle" should be removed by AI.
