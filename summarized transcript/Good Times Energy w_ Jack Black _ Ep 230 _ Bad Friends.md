# Good Times Energy w/ Jack Black | Ep 230 | Bad Friends

## Summary
Bobby Lee and Andrew Santino welcome Jack Black to the Bad Friends podcast for a freewheeling, often crude conversation about comedy, acting, music, and life. The episode is built around riffing rather than a structured interview.

## Highlights
- **The "Tony Hardy" comparison**: Bobby earnestly thanks Jack Black for "paving the road to nookie town" — claims women hook up with him because of the "Jack Black look." Jack calls it the highest compliment he's ever received. Bobby is told his comedic-body equivalent is Tom Hardy.
- **Jack as a teenage thespian**: His turn into music began when he scraped the high notes auditioning for the musical *Pippin* and a girl in the queue gave him "the look" — that's when he realized he could work with this.
- **Sean Penn & Dead Man Walking**: Jack got a tiny role through director Tim Robbins (his first showbiz job came from Robbins's Actors' Gang theater). On set, he watched Penn shoot the cathartic confession scene with Susan Sarandon. Penn was deep in method, "a little unpredictable like a wild animal."
- **Star-struck story**: Jack froze in an elevator at the Trump Soho when his football hero Thierry Henry walked in. Said nothing.
- **Smashburger debate**: Bobby champions HiHo Cheeseburger; Jack defends the Apple Pan in West LA. Tangent on Hanano in Venice — the cafe with wood-chip floors where Jim Morrison reportedly used to sing.
- **Skating**: Jack used to have a mini-ramp in his backyard, took it down after injuries; admits he can't ollie or kickflip.
- **Channeling exercise**: Jack performs an absurd improv "channeling" exercise (red leather yellow leather, "some shun sunshine, do you shun sunshine"). Bobby and Andrew try to copy and Bobby is accused of being a "channel thief."
- **Bombing in the South**: Bobby tells Jack about getting destroyed at a 10,000-person show after Big Jay Oakerson killed. Jack's advice for festivals where people came to see the headliner: "Don't forget to pretend to have a good time" — eventually you actually will.
- **Borderlands**: Jack plays Claptrap in Eli Roth's *Borderlands*. Bobby auditioned, almost panicked because his manager mistakenly told him he had to read for 20 different roles in the table read (it was actually two).
- **Minecraft movie**: Jack confirms he's in Jared Hess's *Minecraft* (with Jason Momoa), live-action + animated, may have written some music.
- **Sobriety check**: Bobby admits he's not happy about being sober, claims it's been 6 or 9 months — he's stopped counting. Andrew nudges him to go back to meetings.

## Tone
Crude, ADD, riff-heavy stand-up-comedian banter. Jack Black is genuinely warm and curious; Bobby and Andrew alternate between worship and roast.
